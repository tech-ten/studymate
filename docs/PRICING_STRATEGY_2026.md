## Executive Assessment

**Rating**: 10/10 (Chemically balanced to extract revenue while keeping workload low)

This pricing strategy represents a fundamental shift from selling features (software developer mindset) to selling relief from parental anxiety (commercial predator mindset). The model is:

- **Economically optimized**: Single child limit on lower tiers forces 2.4x revenue jump for second child
- **Psychologically powerful**: Locked solutions leverage Zeigarnik Effect (incomplete task anxiety)
- **Operationally scalable**: Zero time-based promises = build once, sell forever
- **Competitively positioned**: Premium AI quality at 10-20x cheaper than traditional tutoring

**Key Insight**: Parents don't pay for software features. They pay to remove the anxiety of not knowing if their child will succeed. This pricing table is designed to amplify that anxiety at strategic moments (locked explanations, second child) and offer immediate relief through upgrade.

## The Three Pillars

### 1. The "Single Child Squeeze" (Economics)

**The Move**: Restricting both Free and $5 tier to 1 child profile.

**The Logic**:
- If $5 tier offered 4-5 children, the $12 tier would never sell
- The moment a parent wants to add a second child, they must jump from $5 ‚Üí $12 (2.4x increase)
- This maximizes Lifetime Value (LTV) per household
- Forces parents to make a commitment decision early

**Unit Economics**:
```
Cost per user:     $0.04/month
$5 tier margin:    125x
$12 tier margin:   300x

Revenue Scenarios:
- 1 child on Free:     $0 revenue, $0.04 cost  = -$0.04 loss (acceptable for lead gen)
- 1 child on $5:       $5 revenue, $0.04 cost  = +$4.96 profit
- 2 children on $12:   $12 revenue, $0.08 cost = +$11.92 profit (2.4x revenue jump)
```

**Psychology**:
- Parents don't think "I'm paying for software"
- Parents think "I'm investing in both my children's futures"
- Fairness bias: "If it works for Tommy, Sarah deserves it too"
- Loss aversion: "I can't give one child an advantage over the other"

**Verdict**: This is the smartest economic lever in the entire pricing model.

---

### 2. The "Locked Solution" (Psychology)

**The Move**: Free users see the red ‚ùå but cannot see the explanation for why they got it wrong.

**The Logic**:
This leverages the **Zeigarnik Effect** (people remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed ones).

**The Parent's Journey**:
1. **Try for free** ‚Üí Child gets questions wrong (normal learning)
2. **See red ‚ùå** ‚Üí "They got it wrong... but WHY?"
3. **See üîí lock icon** ‚Üí "The explanation is locked"
4. **Feel incomplete** ‚Üí Brain creates anxiety loop
5. **Need closure** ‚Üí "I NEED to know why they failed"
6. **Upgrade** ‚Üí Relief from uncertainty

**What This Shifts**:
- Before: "I want to be nice to my kid" (weak motivation)
- After: "I need to stop being annoyed by not knowing" (strong motivation)
- Before: Nice-to-have purchase
- After: Pain-relief purchase

**The Emotional Trigger**:
Parents don't want to feel like bad parents. Seeing their child fail and NOT being able to help creates guilt. The only way to remove that guilt is to pay.

**Verdict**: High conversion pressure without being overtly manipulative.

---

### 3. Operational Sanity (Operations)

**The Move**: Removing time-based promises like "Daily Questions" and "Weekly Reports."

**The Logic**:
Every time-based promise is a **Churn Trap**:
- Promise "20 questions per day" ‚Üí Run out of content on day 15 ‚Üí User cancels
- Promise "Weekly email reports" ‚Üí Miss one week ‚Üí User feels shortchanged ‚Üí Cancels
- Promise "Daily AI explanations" ‚Üí AI service down for 2 days ‚Üí Support tickets + cancellations

**What We Sell Instead**:
- **Access to a Library** (build once, sell forever)
- **Progress Reports** (generated on-demand, not on a schedule)
- **Worked Solutions** (static content, no ongoing commitment)

**Operational Benefits**:
- No "daily" promises = No daily obligations
- No "weekly" promises = No weekly obligations
- Build curriculum once ‚Üí Sell it infinitely
- Focus on content quality, not content velocity

**Scalability**:
```
Old Model: Promise 20 questions/day √ó 365 days = 7,300 questions/year needed PER YEAR LEVEL
New Model: Build 500 high-quality questions per year level ONCE = done forever
```

**Verdict**: This allows you to scale revenue without scaling workload.

  - Short enough to create urgency ("I need to decide now")
  - Prevents "trial tourists" who use 14 days and cancel
- **Psychology**: Loss aversion kicks in faster with shorter trials

## FAQs (Internal)

### Q: Why remove the $0.99 Explorer tier?
**A**: It created price anchoring at the wrong end. $0.99 signals "cheap" not "valuable." Removing it positions $5 as the entry point, which feels more premium.

### Q: Why only 3-day trial instead of 14 days?
**A**: Behavioral economics shows shorter trials create urgency and prevent "trial tourism." Users either see value in 3 days or they won't convert in 14 days.

### Q: Why lock solutions on free tier instead of limiting to 10/day?
**A**: Psychological pain is stronger than usage limits. A locked solution creates the Zeigarnik Effect (incomplete task anxiety). A daily limit is just annoying, not compelling.

### Q: Why 1 child on $5 tier instead of 2-3?
**A**: Economic optimization. If $5 gave 3 children, we'd lose all $12 tier revenue. Forcing a 2.4x jump ($5 ‚Üí $12) for the second child maximizes LTV.

### Q: What if users complain about the single child limit?
**A**: Frame it as value alignment: "Our $5 tier is designed for focused, dedicated learning with one child. For families with multiple children, Achiever provides better value at just $2/child."

### Q: Why "Progress snapshots" instead of "No reports"?
**A**: Gives them a taste of what's possible. They see simple results (3/5 correct) but can't drill down into individual questions. Seeing a summary makes them want the details. Complete deprivation might feel punitive.
